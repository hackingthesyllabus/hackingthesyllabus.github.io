What I offer

These workshops are designed to be disarming without being superficial. They create a structured way for people to think together, name what is happening, and leave with clarity that can actually be implemented.

Serious play inspired method

I use a serious play inspired approach that often includes LEGO and other simple games. The point is not play for its own sake. The point is to lower defenses so groups can surface what is normally hard to say, explore possibilities more freely, and move toward change, vision, or healing in a way that feels safe and grounded.

Why it works

Building and sharing makes assumptions visible. It also creates a shared object to talk about. That shift helps people move from blame and debate toward clarity, curiosity, and action.

Important note

This work draws inspiration from serious play style facilitation. It does not claim LEGO Serious Play certification.

Formats

Sessions can be two hours, three hours, half day, full day, or a three day engagement that brings in different stakeholder groups. Group sizes can be four to twenty. The ideal size is eight to twelve.

Workflow

We start with a short discovery conversation. I then design the session structure and any light prework. We run the workshop. You leave with clear themes and next steps that people can carry forward.

Documentation options

Documentation can include a summary of themes, a management ready synthesis, and optional photo or video based documentation for the right use cases. Scope depends on your needs and the session format.

Pricing

Pricing depends on duration, preparation, how many stakeholder groups are involved, and what documentation you want coming out of the session.

Engagements typically range from 1,500 to 15,000 CAD depending on scope.

If you email me a quick description of the goal, group size, and timeline, I will suggest a format and a clear quote.

About

Ikee Gibson is a PhD candidate whose work is grounded in organizational change and in how organizations reinterpret demographic change. This approach draws on theoretical and empirical research in organizational development, organizational culture, work stratification and markets, and equity, diversity, and inclusion, with attention to causal complexity and endogeneity. The goal is to help organizations think together with clarity and momentum.
